# Track Selection

## Track elegido

- Internal: `ai_startup_pitch`
- Official: `04 - Climate Risk & Disaster Coordination`

## Rationale

The official buildathon emphasizes deployable AI systems for real-world
coordination problems in Caribbean economies. Climate disruption is the most
credible wedge because it creates urgent, repeated and cross-sector coordination
work across emergency teams, hotels, ports, clinics, suppliers, NGOs and public
communications.

## Startup thesis

Caribbean T0 Resilience Network reduces the time between alert, decision, owner
and execution. The first product is not a generic chatbot. It is an operating
network that converts fragmented inputs into an auditable incident ledger,
agent-ranked tasks, escalation paths, economic impact and stakeholder-ready
briefs.

## Buyer and user

- Primary buyer: hotels, ports, insurers, utilities, NGOs and emergency
  operations partners that pay for continuity, coordination and compliance.
- Primary user: operations leads who need current status, owners, evidence and
  shareable briefs during time-sensitive disruption.

## Why this beats a normal dashboard

- It outputs actions, not only charts.
- It makes the T=0 promise measurable: baseline minutes, compressed minutes and
  at-risk economic drag.
- It tracks evidence and uncertainty.
- It keeps humans in approval loops.
- It can start with public data and synthetic inventories, then expand into
  partner connectors during pilots.
